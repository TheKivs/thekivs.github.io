About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate of Mathematical Physics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, with a Machine Learning specialization. My goal is to develop ML tools that are robust, reliable and ready for real-world deployment. Specifically, my research has focused on two broad themes: developing a precise understanding of the functioning of widely-used ML techniques; and avenues to make ML methods robust and secure from an adversarial viewpoint.

Prior to this, I was an undergraduate at the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, majoring in Engineering Physics. I also have held research positions with the ML department at CERN, with the Quantum Computing group at the University of Milan and at the University of Kyoto. In a previous life, I competed in Physics and Mathematics olympiads, and represented Illinois in Squash.
